# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

This README centralizes OctoAcme program and project management process documentation. It provides a brief summary of the core processes and direct links to the full documents in the docs/ folder.

OctoAcme follows a clear project lifecycle: Initiation, Planning, Execution, Release, and Retrospective. Projects begin with a lightweight validation step (a Project One‑pager and stakeholder alignment) to confirm desired outcomes and resources. Approved initiatives move into planning where work is decomposed into shippable increments, acceptance criteria are defined, scope is estimated, and milestones and dependencies are mapped. The planning phase also identifies risks and resource needs to ensure the team can deliver against the agreed timeline.

During execution, teams run iterative delivery using a project board (Backlog → Ready → In Progress → In Review → QA → Done), enforce PR and CI conventions (small PRs, acceptance criteria in PRs, automated tests and security scans), and maintain a regular cadence of daily standups, weekly delivery syncs, and demos at the end of sprints or milestones. Releases follow defined types (patch, minor, major) with pre-release checks, a deployment checklist, and rollback/incident playbooks to reduce production risk.

Risk management and continuous improvement are built into the process. The team maintains a risk register with clear owners and mitigation plans and follows an escalation path (team → PM → Product Lead → Sponsor) for business‑impacting issues. Retrospectives capture learnings and produce prioritized action items that are tracked in the backlog. Roles are explicit — Product Managers (define outcomes & measure success), Project Managers (coordinate delivery & communication), Developers (implement & test), QA (validate acceptance), and Stakeholders (provide input & approvals) — so everyone knows responsibilities throughout the lifecycle.
